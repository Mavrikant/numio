# GCD and LCM Calculator

## What Is GCD and LCM?

The Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) is the largest number that divides both numbers.
The Least Common Multiple (LCM) is the smallest number that is a multiple of both.

## Euclidean Algorithm

GCD is computed using the Euclidean algorithm:
GCD(a, b) = GCD(b, a mod b) until b = 0

Then LCM can be found:
LCM(a, b) = (a × b) / GCD(a, b)

## Examples

### Example 1: GCD and LCM of 12 and 18
GCD(12, 18) = 6
LCM(12, 18) = 36

### Example 2: Coprime Numbers (7 and 11)
GCD(7, 11) = 1
LCM(7, 11) = 77

## FAQ

**Q: What is the Euclidean algorithm?**
A: An efficient method for computing GCD by repeated division.

**Q: How are GCD and LCM related?**
A: GCD(a, b) × LCM(a, b) = a × b
