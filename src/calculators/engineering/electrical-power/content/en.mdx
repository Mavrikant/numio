# Electrical Power Calculator: P=VI

## What is Electrical Power?

Electrical power (P) is the rate at which electrical energy is transferred in a circuit, measured in watts (W). It represents how much work is being done per unit time.

**Formula:** P = V × I (voltage times current)

Also derived: P = I² × R (current squared times resistance) and P = V² / R (voltage squared divided by resistance)

## Key Concepts

- **Voltage (V)**: Electric potential difference, measured in volts
- **Current (I)**: Flow of charge, measured in amperes
- **Resistance (R)**: Opposition to current flow, measured in ohms
- **Power (P)**: Rate of energy transfer, measured in watts

## Energy Consumption

Energy = Power × Time (in hours)
Monthly cost = Energy (kWh) × Electricity rate ($/kWh)

This calculator uses a default US average rate of $0.15/kWh. Your actual rate varies by location and utility company.

## Common Applications

- Calculating household appliance consumption
- Estimating electricity bills
- Designing electrical circuits
- Understanding power requirements
