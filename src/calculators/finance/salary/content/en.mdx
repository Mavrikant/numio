## What Is a Take-Home Pay Calculator?

A take-home pay calculator estimates how much of your gross salary you will actually receive in your paycheck after federal income tax, Social Security, Medicare, and state income taxes are withheld. It also accounts for pre-tax deductions — such as 401(k) contributions or health insurance premiums — which reduce the amount of income subject to tax.

Understanding your take-home pay helps you budget accurately, negotiate salary offers, and plan for major expenses. The difference between your gross salary and your net pay can be substantial — often 25–40% for middle-income earners in the United States.

**Key outputs this calculator provides:**
- **Annual take-home pay:** Your yearly net pay after all taxes and deductions
- **Monthly take-home pay:** Your net pay divided by 12
- **Federal income tax:** Based on 2024 progressive tax brackets
- **Social Security tax:** 6.2% on wages up to $168,600
- **Medicare tax:** 1.45% on all wages, plus 0.9% surtax above $200,000
- **State income tax:** Calculated at the flat rate you provide
- **Total tax:** Sum of all taxes withheld
- **Effective tax rate:** Your actual total tax as a percentage of gross salary

## How to Use the Salary Calculator

1. **Enter your annual salary:** Your gross annual salary (before any taxes or deductions).
2. **Select your filing status:** Your federal tax filing status. Common options:
   - **Single:** Unmarried taxpayers
   - **Married Filing Jointly:** Married couples who file a joint return (lower tax brackets)
   - **Head of Household:** Unmarried taxpayers who support a qualifying dependent and pay more than half the household costs
3. **Enter your state income tax rate:** The flat tax rate your state charges on income. Some states (like Texas, Florida, Nevada) have no income tax (enter 0). Others range from about 1% (North Dakota) to 13.3% (California top rate).
4. **Enter pre-tax deductions:** Annual contributions to tax-advantaged accounts that are deducted before tax is calculated. Common examples: 401(k) contributions, traditional IRA deductions, health insurance premiums paid by the employee.
5. **Review your results:** The calculator breaks down every tax component and shows your net annual and monthly pay.

## How the Taxes Are Calculated

### Federal Income Tax (2024 Brackets)

The US federal income tax is progressive — higher portions of income are taxed at higher rates. The tax is applied to **taxable income** (gross salary minus pre-tax deductions), not to your full gross salary.

**2024 Single Filer Brackets:**
| Taxable Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $11,600 | 10% |
| $11,601 – $47,150 | 12% |
| $47,151 – $100,525 | 22% |
| $100,526 – $191,950 | 24% |
| $191,951 – $243,725 | 32% |
| $243,726 – $609,350 | 35% |
| Over $609,350 | 37% |

**2024 Married Filing Jointly Brackets:**
| Taxable Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $23,200 | 10% |
| $23,201 – $94,300 | 12% |
| $94,301 – $201,050 | 22% |
| $201,051 – $383,900 | 24% |
| $383,901 – $487,450 | 32% |
| $487,451 – $731,200 | 35% |
| Over $731,200 | 37% |

### Social Security Tax (FICA)

- Rate: **6.2%** on wages
- 2024 wage base: **$168,600** (wages above this limit are not subject to Social Security tax)
- Example: $80,000 salary → $80,000 × 6.2% = $4,960

### Medicare Tax

- Rate: **1.45%** on all wages (no income ceiling)
- Additional Medicare surtax: **0.9%** on wages above $200,000 (single filers) or $250,000 (married filing jointly)
- Example: $80,000 salary → $80,000 × 1.45% = $1,160

### State Income Tax

This calculator uses a flat rate, which is an approximation of many states' income tax systems. For simplicity:
- Enter the marginal or blended rate for your state
- Most states range from 0% to 10%
- Some states use graduated brackets (like California or New York); for those, use your estimated average rate

### Pre-Tax Deductions

Common pre-tax deductions that reduce your federal and state taxable income:
- **401(k) contributions:** Up to $23,000 in 2024 ($30,500 if age 50+)
- **Traditional IRA:** Up to $7,000 in 2024 ($8,000 if age 50+)
- **Health insurance premiums** (if paid through an employer section 125 cafeteria plan)
- **Health Savings Account (HSA):** Up to $4,150 (individual) or $8,300 (family) in 2024

## FAQ

**What is the difference between marginal and effective tax rates?**
Your **marginal tax rate** is the rate applied to your last dollar of income (e.g., 22% for a single filer earning $75,000). Your **effective tax rate** is the total tax you pay as a percentage of your gross income — it is always lower than your marginal rate because lower portions of income are taxed at lower rates. For a $75,000 single filer, the effective federal rate is closer to 15%.

**Does this calculator include the standard deduction?**
No — this calculator focuses on employment income and uses pre-tax deductions (like 401k) as the tax reducer. The standard deduction ($14,600 for single filers in 2024, $29,200 for married) further reduces federal taxable income. For a more complete picture, subtract the standard deduction from your taxable income before interpreting results.

**What states have no income tax?**
As of 2024: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire (on wages), South Dakota, Tennessee (on wages), Texas, Washington, Wyoming. Enter 0% for these states.

**Does my employer's portion of FICA affect my take-home pay?**
No. Your employer also pays 6.2% Social Security and 1.45% Medicare on your behalf, but this does not reduce your paycheck — it is an additional cost to the employer. This calculator shows only the employee portion.

**What about self-employment tax?**
Self-employed individuals pay both the employee and employer portions of FICA (totaling 15.3% on net self-employment income, up to the wage base). This calculator is designed for W-2 employees.
